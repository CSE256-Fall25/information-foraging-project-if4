Curriculum Overview

A thorough explanation of the WUNS Curriculum Overview that outlines the school's child-centered, process-oriented, play-based philosophy, which is built on Constructivist theories and guided by educational theorists like Piaget and Vygotsky. The curriculum addresses the developmental needs of the whole child through a learning center approach, with a focus on:

  • Social/Emotional Development: Building self-esteem, collaboration, and respect for self and others.
  • Intellectual Development: Enhancing language, forming math skills (counting, sorting, graphing) , and developing science (observation, recycling) and social studies concepts (culture, community, occupations).
  • Physical Development: Developing gross motor skills (climbing, kicking, hiking) and fine motor skills (writing, using manipulatives, stringing beads).
  • Visual Arts: Emphasizing the process of exploration with materials like paint, clay, and printmaking.
  • Assessment: Details the ongoing teacher observation and planning, including the purpose and format of the Fall (informal) and Spring (formal) parent/teacher conferences.
